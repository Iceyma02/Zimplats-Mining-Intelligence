📖 Nine Years in One Paragraph
Zimplats produced 5.18 million platinum group metal ounces between FY2017 and FY2025, generating $8.0 billion in cumulative revenue. The company transformed from a $512M operation to briefly touching $1.35 billion in FY2021 as PGM prices surged — then watched revenue compress 43% even while production hit a record 646,000 oz in FY2024. FY2025 marks a critical inflection: production fell for the first time in three years, safety deteriorated to a 6-year low, and costs hit their highest point ever — while a $1.8 billion capital programme nears completion.

Production Intelligence
Ore mined, milled, head grade, and 6E ounce output across all 9 years. From the 6-year milling plateau to the Third Concentrator breakthrough — and the FY2025 TMM-driven reversal.
📖 The Production Story
From FY2017 to FY2022 — six consecutive years — Zimplats milled between 6.5 and 6.9 million tonnes despite rising CapEx every year. The concentrators were at their ceiling. The breakthrough came in September 2022 with the Third Concentrator (0.9Mtpa), pushing milling to 7.5Mt in FY2023 and a record 7.9Mt in FY2024. Then FY2025 reversed — milling fell to 7.5Mt. The CEO named the cause explicitly: reduced TMM engineering availability underground. Equipment broke. Mines couldn't load and haul ore. And there was no predictive system to prevent it.

Financial Intelligence

⚠ Financial Alert — Core Vulnerability
Between FY2021 and FY2024, production grew 11% (579k → 646k oz) yet revenue fell 43% ($1.35B → $767M). This is the defining financial reality: 70% of Zimplats' performance is determined by global PGM prices, not by operational decisions. Palladium collapsed from $2,400/oz to under $900. Rhodium from $20,000 to $4,000. Meanwhile operating costs rose 46% — squeezing gross margins from 59.6% (FY2021) to 32.8% (FY2025). The only protection is operational efficiency. That's precisely what's missing.

Safety Intelligence
Nine years of LTIFR, TIFR, LTI counts, and fatalities. A system that only measures harm after it happens — and a safety performance that is structurally volatile across the full dataset.
🚨 Critical Alert — FY2025 Safety Crisis
FY2024 achieved the best safety record in company history: LTIFR 0.13, only 3 LTIs. Twelve months later: LTIFR 0.68, 13 LTIs — a 423% deterioration. This is the fifth LTIFR spike above 0.40 in nine years. The same pattern precedes every spike: contractor numbers surge, safety culture absorption fails, incidents follow 6–12 months later. Zimplats measures safety exclusively through lagging indicators. There is no predictive layer. No early warning. No leading indicator system. The FY2025 crisis was visible in the FY2024 data — but no one was watching the right signal.

📋 Safety Event Timeline — All 9 Years
FY2017 — Safest Year to That Point
LTIFR 0.21 · TIFR 0.41 · 0 fatalities · 6 LTIs. Contractors at 2,878 — lowest in dataset. Over 6.84M fatality-free shifts. Strong safety culture when workforce is stable and experienced.
FY2018 — New Safety Record Set
LTIFR 0.19 — best ever at the time · 0 fatalities · only 3 LTIs. The gold standard Zimplats would not match again until FY2024. Bimha Mine at design capacity. Experienced, stable workforce across all mines.
FY2019 — First Fatality + Major LTI Spike
LTIFR 0.45 · TIFR 0.90 (9yr high) · 1 fatality · 12 LTIs. Fatal accident at Mupfuti Mine in April 2019. Contractors grew to 3,791. TIFR of 0.90 remains the single highest reading across all 9 years. Fatality-free sequence broken at 10.2M shifts.
FY2020 — LTIFR Worsens Despite Zero Fatalities
LTIFR 0.59 · TIFR 0.65 · 0 fatalities · 9 LTIs. Worst injury frequency rate in the 9-year period. COVID disrupted supervision and safety protocols. Contractors fell to 2,798 — the improvement would show 12 months later in FY2021.
FY2021 — Near-Best Safety Year
LTIFR 0.20 · TIFR 0.46 · 1 fatality · only 2 LTIs. Best injury frequency since FY2018. Contractors at lowest since FY2017. 1 fatal accident in February. Extended fatality-free sequences across multiple mines.
FY2022 — Contractor Surge Begins Safety Decline
LTIFR 0.30 · TIFR 0.56 · 1 fatality · 6 LTIs. Contractors jumped 66% to 5,225 as capital projects peaked. Fatal conveyor belt accident at Bimha Mine (March 2022). The deterioration that would peak in FY2023 and again in FY2025 was starting here.
FY2023 — Worst Fatality Year: 2 Deaths
LTIFR 0.35 · TIFR 0.55 · 2 fatalities · 7 LTIs. First year in the dataset with two fatalities. Third Concentrator commissioning brought new workers to high-risk environments. Company called it a "painful reminder." No systematic change in approach followed.
FY2024 — Breakthrough: Best-Ever Safety Performance
LTIFR 0.13 · TIFR 0.45 · 1 fatality · only 3 LTIs. Most dramatic single-year safety improvement in company history. Contractors fell to 4,790. Extended fatality-free sequences. This result was real — but not deeply analysed. What changed? That question went unanswered. It cost dearly in FY2025.
FY2025 — Sharp Reversal: 13 LTIs
LTIFR 0.68 · TIFR 1.00 · 0 fatalities · 13 LTIs — 333% increase in incidents. Contractors back at ~5,000. TMM maintenance crisis forced non-standard procedures, increasing exposure risk. Zero fatalities is a positive — but 13 injured workers in one year signals systemic safety failure as the workforce expanded without safety intelligence to match.

📖 Workforce Story
Zimplats employed 5,943 people in FY2017. By FY2025 total workforce exceeded 9,150 — a 54% increase. But growth was structurally uneven: permanent employees grew 35% while contractors grew 74%. Contractors now represent over 55% of the total workforce. This matters enormously: contractor safety outcomes are worse, productivity is lower, and revenue per employee halved from $382K (FY2021) to $199K (FY2025) — a productivity collapse driven by both PGM price decline and workforce expansion without equivalent output growth.

Strategic Intelligence
Cross-cutting insights from 9 years of data. These findings don't appear in any single annual report — they emerge when you read the full dataset. This is what intelligence looks like.
◈ Intelligence Summary
Nine years of data reveals three structural problems Zimplats cannot solve with effort alone: TMM failures that are predictable but unmonitored, safety volatility that follows a contractor pattern with no early warning system to intercept it, and costs rising without granular visibility into where the leakage occurs. The $1.8B capital programme is nearly complete. What comes next is the operational efficiency era — and that requires intelligence, not just capital.
Finding #1 — TMM & Production Loss Pattern
Every year where ore milling stagnated or declined traces back to equipment availability. FY2025 is the most explicit — TMM named by the CEO directly. $41M in estimated lost revenue from unmonitored equipment.
HIGHEST PRIORITY
Finding #2 — Safety Lag Pattern (6–12 Month Lead Time)
Contractor count predicts LTIFR with a 6–12 month lag. Visible every time in 9 years of data. FY2025's crisis was predictable from FY2024's contractor numbers. A leading indicator system would have flagged it months earlier.
